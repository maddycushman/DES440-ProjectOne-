EIGHTIES

Whatcho talkin’ bout Willis?

They call it the decade that made us. It was a decade about the people, decisions,and inventions that changed the future of our daily lives. From Pac-Man and blockbusters, to NASA and Reagan, scroll down to take an epic journey through an unforgettable era.

'80

Pac-Man

IT ALL STARTED WITH PIZZA...

To call Pac-Man a mere game would be an injustice. It’s a cultural icon and has continued to be loved for generations. Like any good idea, it started with pizza. Toru Iwatani claims that he was staring at a pizza when he had the idea for a video game centered around eating. Today, the game is cited as the highest grossing video game of all time.

The Shining

REDRUM, REDRUM, REDRUM

The iconic horror film directed by Stanley Kubrick ended up being a horror on and off the screen. The movie is about supernatural forces putting pressure on an already fractured family. There was claustrophobia and agoraphobia on and off the screen as Kubrick made the cast feel just as crazy as the characters they were playing. If fact, it was so strenuous, Shelley Duvall became physically ill from the stress of shooting.

'81

Ronald Reagan

"We can't help everyone,
but everyone can help someone."

By the time of the 1980 election, the nation was wracked by high inflation and unemployment, gasoline shortages, and a hostage crisis in Iran. Ronald Regan urged Americans to believe in themselves again and declared that the United States was a "shining city on a hill" whose best days were still ahead and he defeated Carter in a landslide.

MTV was born

“Ladies and gentlemen, rock and roll.”

The Buggles’ “Video Killed the Radio Star” was the first music video to air on the new cable television channel, which initially was available only to households in parts of New Jersey. As the years would go by, MTV was instrumental in promoting the careers of performers such as Madonna, Michael Jackson, Prince and Duran Duran, whose videos played in heavy rotation.

'82

E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial

E.T. phone home.

This science fiction fantasy film, directed by Steven Spielberg, tells the story of a lonely boy who befriends an extraterrestrial, dubbed "E.T.", who is stranded on Earth. E.T. was an immediate blockbuster, and was nominated for nine Academy Awards. It was acclaimed by critics as a timeless story of friendship.

Thriller

Darkness falls across the land...

This nearly 14-minute-long musical horror film was letting Michael Jackson indulge his monster-movie fantasies. At the time, it was the most expensive music video to ever be produced, but it set a new benchmark for blockbusters that changed how the music business promoted and marketed superstar releases. Jackson moonwalked his way into music history.

'83

First American Female in Space

All aboard the Challenger!

On June 18, 1983, she became the first American woman in space as a crew member on space shuttle Challenger for STS-7. She was preceded by two Soviet women, Valentina Tereshkova in 1963 and Svetlana Savitskaya in 1982.

Goodbye M.A.S.H.

The war is over!

In 1983, the beloved show M.A.S.H. came to an end. Members of the 4077th Mobile Army Surgical Hospital care for the injured during the Korean War and use humor to escape from the horror and depression of the situation. The show became one of the most popular tv shows in history.

'84

Apple Personal Computer

Thanks Steve Jobs!

Apple introduces the Macintosh with a television commercial during the 1984 Super Bowl, which plays on the theme of totalitarianism in George Orwell´s book 1984. The ad featured the destruction of “Big Brother” – a veiled reference to IBM -- through the power of personal computing found in a Macintosh.

Purple Rain

I only wanted to see you...

Purple Rain is a 1984 American rock musical drama film directed by Albert Magnoli, staring and showcase Prince and his musical talents. Prince plays "the Kid" and navigates the club scene and a rocky relationship with a captivating singer, Apollonia. The film won an Academy Award for Best Original Song Score, the last to receive the award.

'85

The Breakfast Club

Don't you forget about me

A brain, an athlete, a basket case, a princess or a criminal, this movie is a classic. Released 30 years ago, John Hughes’ classic film transcends generations and continues to reach new fans today. The story follows five teens, each members of different high school cliques, who spend a day in detention and come to see that they are all more than their stereotypes.

Nintendo Entertainment System

NES is an 8-bit home video game console that was developed and manufactured by Nintendo. It was initially released in Japan. This was following a series of arcade game successes in the early 1980s, the first few games realesed with the console was Donkey Kong, Donkey Kong Jr. and Popeye.

'86

Chernobyl Disaster

A catastrophic nuclear accident

The Chernobyl accident dominates as one of most disastrous nuclear power plant accident in history, both in terms of cost and casualties. During the accident, blast effects caused 2 deaths within the facility, later 134 were hospitalized with acute radiation symptoms, of which 28 firemen and employees died in the days afterward from the effects radiation. And there were approximately 14 cancer deaths amongst this group in the next ten years.

Challenger exploded

"We will never forget them."

On January 28, 1986, the American shuttle orbiter Challenger broke up 73 seconds after liftoff, bringing a devastating end to the spacecraft’s 10th mission. The disaster claimed the lives of all seven astronauts aboard, including Christa McAuliffe, a teacher from New Hampshire who had been selected to join the mission and teach lessons from space to schoolchildren around the country.

'87

Dirty Dancing

No one puts baby in a corner

There’s something about the innocence of the film, which shows love blossom between a teenager and a dance instructor during the summer of 1963, that made it a timeless classic. The story centers around the resort's dance instructor, Johnny, when he enlists Baby as his new partner, and then the two fall in love. It has become one of the best dance movies to ever be made.

Full House

You got it, dude.

The classic american sitcom centers around the events of widowed father, Danny Tanner, who enlists his brother-in-law and best friend to help raise his three daughters. It has become a favorite amoungs generations and loved by so many people. Recently in 2017, made a comeback with Fuller House on Netflix.

'88

A Brief History
of Time

By the one and only, Stephen Hawking

A landmark volume in science writing by one of the great minds of our time, Hawking’s book explores such profound questions as: How did the universe begin—and what made its start possible? Is the universe unending—or are there boundaries? Are there other dimensions? What will happen when it all ends?

George Bush

"Equality begins with economic empowerment."

After president Regan served his two terms, the next presidential race was between George H. W. Bush and Michael Dukakis . Bush capitalized on a good economy and Reagan's popularity. Meanwhile, Dukakis's campaign suffered from several miscues, including failure to defend against Bush's attacks. This allowed Bush to win with a substantial margin of the popular vote

'89

Berlin Wall

The Fall of the Wall

The official purpose of this Berlin Wall was to keep Western “fascists” from entering East Germany and undermining the socialist state. On November 9, 1989, as the Cold War began to thaw across Eastern Europe, the spokesman for East Berlin's Communist Party announced a change in his city's relations with the West. Starting at midnight that day, he said, citizens of the GDR were free to cross the country's borders.

Back to the Future

Great Scott!

Back to the Future is a 1985 American science-fiction adventure comedy film[6] directed by Robert Zemeckis and written by Zemeckis and Bob Gale. The story centers around a teenager Marty McFly, who travels back in time with an eccentric scientist to 1955 , where he meets his future parents in high school and accidentally becomes his mother's romantic interest.
